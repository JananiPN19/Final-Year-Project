TOEFL ( Test of English as a Foreign Language )


TOEFL otherwise known as Test of English as a Foreign Language. Lets see the structure and pattern of TOEFL for you to get more knowledge about TOEFL.

Reading:


The TOEFL Reading section is very similar to the RC section in GRE, with the only difference being that this one is VERY simple. There can be two formats in Reading: Short format or Long format.

Short format:
It consists of 3 passages each approximately 700 words in length and with 10 questions and overall time is 54 minutes. The passages are on academic topics.

Long format
It is 72 minutes duration and has 4 passages each approximately 700 words in length and with 10 questions. Of the 4 passages only 3 will be evaluated to get your score, the other 1 are experimental and do not matter at all. However, you have no idea which ones will matter and which ones won’t, so you have to give your best in all.

Listening:


The TOEFL Listening section is a section for most of the students and it tests the student’s listening skills. In this section you have to listen to entire conversations (A conversation involves two speakers, a student and either a professor or a campus service provider) or lectures at one go (which can be very boring). Again there can be two formats: Short or Long format.

Short format:
It consists of two conversations and three lectures each having 5-6 questions. Overall duration is 25-30 minutes.

Long format:
It consists of three conversations and four lectures each having 5-6 questions, of which two conversations and four lectures only will contribute to your score. The remaining are redundant but again you are kept in the dark about this, so give your best in all. Overall duration is 100 minutes.

Note:

If you get Short format in Reading, you will get Long format in Listening or the other way round.

Speaking:


The TOEFL Speaking section tests the ability to communicate in English in an academic setting. It has four tasks. Tasks 1 will prompt you to speak on some familiar topic like a personal experience. You are given 10-15 seconds to think and another 30 seconds to speak on each of the questions. Tasks 2 & 3 require you to read a paragraph, followed by listening to a short conversation and then respond to a question about what you read and heard. In the next one question (campus situation) you have to listen to an audio clip, remember the important points in it and then answer the questions based on it in 45-60 seconds. Overall duration of this section is 17 minutes.


Writing:

Although the TOEFL Writing section is quite a different section, it should be comfortable section for those who have already appeared for GRE. You are required to write two essays: Integrated Essay and Independent Essay.

Integrated Essay:
It consists of two conversations and three lectures each having 5-6 questions. Overall duration is 25-30 minutes.

Independent Essay:
In the independent task, you must write an essay that states, explains, and supports your opinion on an issue, supporting your opinions or choices, rather than simply listing personal preferences or choices. You are given 30 minutes for writing this in about 300-350 words.

What is GRE?


The Graduate Record Examination (GRE) Revised General Test is a test that is a requirement for most of the masters and doctoral degree programs in most of the universities of the US, UK, Germany, and Canada. The exams are developed and administered by Educational Testing Services (ETS).

What are the prerequisites for GRE?


There are no official prerequisites for taking this exam and it is intended for undergraduate students and bachelor’s degree graduated students. It is offered almost everywhere in the world in only one language- English.

There are no official prerequisites for taking this exam and it is intended for undergraduate students and bachelor’s degree graduated students. It is offered almost everywhere in the world in only one language- English.

What’s the purpose of GRE Exam?


According to the ETS, the GRE intends to measure the analytical, quantitative, verbal, and critical thinking skills of a test-taker. Broadly speaking, the GRE questions are based on concepts from the basic Maths, verbal logic, and vocabulary.

What’s the cost for GRE Exam?


The cost of taking the test is 205 US Dollars (Although, it might vary based on the country). ETS also offers financial aid to students who prove economic hardship.

GRE Subject Tests


In addition to the GRE Revised General Test, ETS also offers GRE Subject Tests that test your knowledge in a particular field of study which include Biology, Chemistry, Literature in English, Mathematics, Physics, and Psychology. It must be noted that, the GRE Subject Tests are not a necessary requirement for admission to universities.

CGPA ( Cumulative Grade Point Average )


CGPA stands for Cumulative Grade Point Average. It is the average of Grade Points obtained in all the subjects in college To know your cgpa percentage multiple by 9.5 with the overall result. When you’re enrolled in a Bachelor’s or Master’s programme, your GPA is easily one of the most important numbers to keep an eye on. 

Your GPA, or Grade Point Average, is a number that indicates how well or how high you scored in your courses on average. It’s meant to score you (usually on a GPA scale between 1.0 and 4.0) during your studies and shows whether your overall grades have been high or low. This number is then used to assess whether you meet the standards and expectations set by the degree programme or university.
